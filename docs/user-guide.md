# GymForge User Guide

Welcome to GymForge! This guide will help you get the most out of your workout tracking.

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Visit the GymForge homepage
2. Click "Get Started" or "Sign Up"
3. Enter your email or sign in with Google/GitHub
4. You're in! Your account is ready to use

### First Steps

After signing in, you'll land on your **Dashboard**. From here, you can:
- Create your first workout plan
- Start logging workouts
- View your progress (once you have data)

## Creating a Workout Plan

Your workout plan defines what exercises you'll do each day of the week.

### Steps to Create a Plan

1. Go to **Plan** from the sidebar
2. Click **Create Plan**
3. Give your plan a name (e.g., "Push/Pull/Legs", "Upper/Lower Split")
4. For each day of the week:
   - Click to expand the day
   - Optionally add a label (e.g., "Push Day")
   - Click **Add Exercise** to add exercises
   - Set the number of sets and target reps for each

### Choosing Exercises

When you click **Add Exercise**, you can:
- **Search** the existing catalog of 40+ exercises
- **Filter** by muscle group or equipment
- **Create custom** exercises if you don't find what you need

## Logging Workouts

### Starting a Workout

1. Go to **Log Workout** from the sidebar
2. You'll see today's planned exercises
3. For each set:
   - Enter the weight you used
   - Enter the reps you completed
   - Click the save button

### Tips for Logging

- **Pre-filled weights**: The app remembers your last weight for each exercise
- **Save as you go**: Click save after each set so you don't lose data
- **Don't worry about perfection**: Log what you actually did, not what you planned

### Completing a Workout

When you're done:
1. Make sure all your sets are saved
2. Click **Complete Workout** at the bottom
3. Your session is marked as complete!

### Rest Days

If today is a rest day in your plan, you'll see a message letting you know. Take the day offâ€”recovery is part of the process!

## Tracking Progress

The **Progress** page shows how your lifts are improving over time.

### Overview Stats

At the top you'll see:
- **Workouts this week**: How many sessions you've logged
- **Weekly volume**: Total weight lifted (sets Ã— reps Ã— weight)
- **Volume trend**: How this week compares to last week
- **Exercises tracked**: Total exercises with data

### Weekly Volume Chart

The main chart shows your total volume week by week. Look for:
- **Upward trends**: You're doing more work over time
- **Sudden spikes**: Might indicate too much too fast
- **Dips**: Could be deloads, rest weeks, or missed sessions

### Exercise Progress

Below the chart, exercises are grouped by muscle group. Click any exercise to see detailed charts:

1. **Top Set Weight**: Your heaviest set over time
2. **Estimated 1RM**: Calculated from your best set (Epley formula)
3. **Volume**: Total work for this exercise per session
4. **Session History**: Table of recent performances

### Understanding Suggestions

GymForge analyzes your data and provides suggestions:

- **"Ready to Progress!"**: You've been consistentâ€”try adding weight
- **"Keep It Up!"**: You're on the right track, keep working
- **"Consider a Deload"**: You might be strugglingâ€”try reducing weight

These are guidelines, not rules. Listen to your body!

## Best Practices

### Consistency is Key

- Log every workout, even if it's not perfect
- Showing up is more important than perfect numbers

### Progressive Overload

The #1 rule for building strength:
- Add a little weight or an extra rep over time
- Don't jump too fastâ€”2.5-5 lbs per week is great progress

### Use Rest Days

- Your muscles grow during recovery, not during workouts
- Plan 2-3 rest days per week

### Trust the Process

- Progress isn't always linear
- Bad days happenâ€”just keep showing up
- Look at trends over weeks and months, not individual sessions

## FAQ

### Can I log workouts for past dates?

Yes! Use the date selector on the Log page to navigate to any date.

### What if I don't follow my plan exactly?

That's fine! The plan is a template. Log what you actually do.

### Can I have multiple plans?

Yes! Create new plans anytime. Only one plan is "active" at a time, but you can switch between them.
